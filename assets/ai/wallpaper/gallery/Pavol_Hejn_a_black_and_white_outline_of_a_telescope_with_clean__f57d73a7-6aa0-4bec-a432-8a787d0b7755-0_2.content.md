## About the Telescope

The telescope has been an essential tool for astronomers since the early 17th century when Galileo first used one to observe the moons of Jupiter. Since then, telescopes have played a vital role in advancing our understanding of the universe. They allow us to see beyond what the naked eye can perceive, revealing the wonders of the cosmos.

Telescopes come in many different types and sizes, each with its own unique capabilities. Some are designed for observing distant galaxies, while others are better suited for studying the planets in our solar system. Regardless of their design, all telescopes share the same basic principle: they use lenses or mirrors to gather and focus light, allowing us to see objects that would otherwise be invisible.
